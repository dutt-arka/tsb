About Tripura

The landscape:
Tripura is a State of nearly four million people located in the North East India and is surrounded on its 3 sides by Bangladesh. [Latitude of Tripura is 23.940848 N & its Longitude is 91.988153 E] .
It was a princely state ruled by its Maharajas over centuries and was never a part of the British India. It merged with the Indian Union on the 15th October, 1949.
The state has a geographical mass of about 10491 sq. km and it shares 856 km long border with Bangladesh. The state is rich in historical and cultural heritage, but suffered a geographical isolation immediately after its merger with India. Weak infrastructure – in particular, the limited road and rail network both within the State and that connecting it to the rest of India – has been a bane for decades which affected its overall development. During the last two decades the people witnessed social strife and suffered from the effects of insurgency and associated violence. But this dark period has since been buried and the state is now surging forward with all round developmental activities.
It experienced significant progress in the social, economic and political indicators of human development. The situation in Tripura is now way ahead compared to many other states, particularly with respect to achievements in health and education sector and in implementation of effective institutions of democratic decentralization. The Tripura State Branch of the Indian Red Cross Society was constituted on the 15th September, 1959 and ever since that day, this Institution has been working steadfastly for amelioration of the people facing distress due to various anthropogenic disturbances.

The demography:


There are 19 vibrant tribes in Tripura who constitute about 31% of total population, rest of whom are Bengali people, many whom had migrated to Tripura during and after partition of India (1947) from erstwhile East Pakistan. Literacy rate of Tripura as per 2011 Census is 87.75%. However, it is placed on record that the literacy rate in Tripura has seen upward trend and is 97.22 percent as per 2016 Literacy Assessment Survey (LAS), of which the male literacy stands at 97.34 percent while female literacy is at 96.79 percent. Tripura has 591 Gram Panchayats where 6111 elected representatives (including 3113 women members) are carrying out the decentralized planning and development activities across the state in addition to 587 vibrant village committees in the Autonomous District Council (ADC) areas where 4597 elected representatives are engaged in translating the government sponsored programmes into reality.
